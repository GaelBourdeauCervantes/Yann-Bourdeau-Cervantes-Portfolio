Yann Bourdeau Cervantes

I am a French-Mexican artist deeply influenced by both cultures, with a lifelong passion for the arts. My artistic journey began at age 8, self-teaching painting, sculpting, and drawing. My formative years were spent primarily at Liceo Franco Mexicano in Mexico City, with educational experiences in Canada and France. I'm also a chess enthusiast with tournament wins, which broadened my worldview. At 14, I ventured into oil painting and pencil sketches, followed by sculptural work related to anatomy, including a collaboration on a surgical animation. I delved into 3D animation, contributing to events in Washington, D.C., and "Francais du Monde" ADFE. Currently, I focus on landscape architecture, studying at the University of Montreal and collaborating on various projects, including animations for the Mexican Consulate in Montreal and Architect Javier Senosiain. My journey is marked by a commitment to continuous learning and an openness to new opportunities.
